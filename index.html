Experience

  1. Product Manager Intern, Adventures in Growth

    June 2023 — August 2023

    For my MBA summer internship, I interned with a pre-seed startup building a community platform to share stories from senior tech executives in the startup world through podcast and newsletter content. While my prior experience had been at a big tech company in Amazon, I was excited to develop my business strategy and product skills in a brand new startup environment.

    During the internship, I identified an opportunity to leverage generative AI to automate significant portions of content creation, which typically takes creators hours of time. While some AI podcast tools existed in the market, all offered relatively standardized outputs, ignoring the personal tone behind authoring original pieces that drives organic podcast growth.

    Therefore, I owned end-to-end creation of a minimum viable product of a generative AI driven podcast tool that could offer more customization towards the author's purpose and audience, and be trained upon prior content created by the user. During the internship, I performed customer interviews, market segmentation analysis, product requirements document, and concept testing.

    At the end of the internship, I had developed a prioritized feature backlog and wireframes for components that could not be implemented during the internship.

  2. Software Development Engineer, Amazon Halo

    January 2022 — August 2022

    I enjoyed the remainder of my time at Amazon, working in the Devices organiation and gaining experience working on an external-facing product: Halo Rise, a bedside sleep tracker and wake light

    In this role, I developed components for sleep data ingestion orchestration framework and led launch readiness activities, such as designing and coordinating load testing to support over 400 TPS, a critical requirement to ensure on-time launch of Halo Rise

  3. Software Development Engineer, Amazon Denied Party Screening

    August 2019 — December 2021

    I started my full-time professional career in Amazon Denied Party Screening. When I joined, the organization was brand new, allowing me to grow rapidly and take on larger responsibilities quicker, including my promotion to SDE2 in under 1.5 years.

    As part of the organization, I spent time designing a microservice architecture built on AWS, including a transition to a real-time machine learning processing system and developing several products' roadmaps for our alert processing system and case management platform. These systems helped reduce manual investigation effort by 90% while increasing system throughput.

    As a tech lead, I also mentored several teammates and interns during the rapid expansion of the engineering team, including leading a brand new team created in India by helping them get up to speed to deliver a data aggregation tool that simplified user experience.

  4. Technical Project Analyst Intern, BAE Systems

    May 2017 — August 2017

    After my first year of undergrad, I accepted a summer internship at BAE Systems where I designed a SQL database to track over 2000 military intelligence officers deployed in Afghanistan from recruitment through redeployment that reduced data entry and processing time by 67%. In addition, I produced data analytics tools that could demonstrate project growth and would be used to display data in future government contract proposals.

  5. Student Research Intern, National Institute of Standards and Technology (NIST)

    June 2015 — August 2015

    Between my junior and senior years of high school, I interned in NIST's Industrial Control Systems Lab to gain practical experience in the fields of computer science and robotics.

    I designed a vision-based system to monitor manufacturing robots’ movements in real-time that provides the ability to quickly detect robot malfunction or deviation from its planned path, and therefore prevent accidents in the workplace.
    My project research paper was named 1 of 300 National Semifinalists in the 2016 Intel Science Talent Search, the nation’s most prestigious pre-college science competition.
